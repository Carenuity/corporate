One‑Pager – IoT & AI Sensor Kits for the Kisii University Innovation Days 2026

Empowering Students with Practical IoT, Electronics & AI Skills

For the Innovation Days at Kisii University on April 7th, ChipGlobe Ltd. i.f. proudly donates 2×20 Carenuity Sensor IoT Boxes. These kits enable 20 students to build their first IoT devices independently within minutes. Each kit comprises 2 boxes, which include the C3‑Mini microcontroller, the ESP32-CAM microcontroller, a curated set of modules like sensors, an OLED display, relay, or buzzer, and the Carenuity Triple‑Adapter, which seamlessly connects sensor, microcontroller, and actuator into many fully functional IoT units.

This donation is currently unique worldwide — no other university has received these boxes in this special educational edition. Kisii University is the first institution globally to receive and present these kits in this form, ahead of their official introduction at:

https://www.carenuity.com/product/assortment-box/c3-mini-kisii-boxes

The IoT kits will be officially presented and handed over by Timothy Mwala, the ChipGlobe representative for Kenya, who plays a key role in establishing the educational and innovation partnership between ChipGlobe Ltd. i.f. and Kisii University.

Students can instantly create devices such as barometers, hygrometers, thermometers, lux meters, IR radiometers, motion detectors or presence sensors — no prior experience required.

Fast, Browser‑Based Development with the Solution Builder

All IoT kits are tightly integrated with the Carenuity Solution Builder, a fully browser‑based development platform. It allows students to build and flash complete applications to the C3‑Mini in under three minutes, without installing software, drivers, or development environments.
Applications are selected, flashed via USB‑C, and ready to run immediately. The platform provides transparent GitHub‑hosted source code and a streamlined hardware‑software integration process designed for both beginners and advanced developers.

This drastically lowers technical barriers and enables students to prototype, test and share ideas within minutes.

The Home Challenge – A Complete Learning Journey

The kits also give students access to the Carenuity Home Challenge, a structured education program covering the full spectrum of IoT skills — from soldering and simulation to PCB design, API integration, app development, 3D enclosure design, data logging and first machine‑learning concepts.
Each level builds on the previous one, offering a clear and motivating progression pathway for students of all backgrounds.

The program is already running in multiple countries and fits perfectly for developing emerging engineering talent in Kenya.
